Indian Sign Language

Indian Sign Language (ISL) is the predominant sign language in the subcontinent of South Asia, used by at least 15 million deaf signers. As with many sign languages, it is difficult to estimate numbers with any certainty, as the Census of India does not list sign languages and most studies have focused on the north and urban areas. As of 2024, it is the most used sign language in the world, and Ethnologue ranks it as the 149th most "spoken" language in the world.

About our Application

Ishaare is an innovative web application designed to bridge communication gaps between the deaf and hard-of-hearing community and the hearing world. Leveraging advanced computer vision and natural language processing technologies, Ishaare offers real-time translation of Indian Sign Language (ISL) into text and speech, enabling seamless and effective communication.

Features:

-Provides instant translation of ISL into text and speech, enabling immediate comprehension and response.

-Access a robust library of ISL signs and gestures, continually updated to include a wide range of vocabulary and phrases.

-Offers translation of ISL into multiple Indian languages, including Hindi, Bengali, Tamil, Punjabi, and more.

-Provides educational resources about sign language and deaf culture to foster greater understanding and inclusivity.
